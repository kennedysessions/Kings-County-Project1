Democrats lose ground in Kings County, Brooklyn

Democrats lost support and voters stayed home in Kings County, Brooklyn, data shows

Brooklyn is known as a blue stronghold for Democrats but data shows more voters decided to stay home instead.

By: Kennedy Sessions, Nov. 17, 2024

According to election results, Republicans made huge gains across the nation in Tuesday's general election, including in areas that traditionally vote blue. While Democrats still won New York's 28 electoral college votes, their support lagged in some of New York City's bluest boroughs.

Although Vice President Kamala Harris overwhelmingly won Kings County, data from the New York State Board of Elections show Democrats lost thousands of supporters when compared to the 2020 Presidential Election.

Out of the 787,709 voters who cast their ballots on Tuesday, 551,633 voted for Harris, while 219,335 voted for Trump. And 16,741 voters decided to go with neighter candidate and write in a third choice instead.

Thousands of people decided to stay home this election with 787,709 Brooklynnites voted casting their ballots while while 920,380 voted in 2024.

Voting results show in 2020, Biden and Harris secured 703,310 votes from Democratic and Working Families Party voters. While Trump and former Vice President Mike Pence secured 202,772 votes from the Republican and Conservative Party voters, an overall lost of 7 percentage points.

Kings County Conservative Party Chair Fran Vella-Marrone described the numbers as a "rightward shift" that's been building since 2021.
